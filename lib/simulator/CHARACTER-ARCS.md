# Dark Mirror — Character Arcs & Story Map

This is the writing reference for everyone touching simulator content.
It maps every character to the scenarios they appear in, the arc they
travel across the track, and the book tactic each beat is teaching.
Use it before writing new scenes so the through-line stays consistent
and characters don't behave out of their established voice.

---

## The protagonist (you)

The female track is a single eight-year arc, told in ten levels. The
player is the same person throughout — the gala-fresh 24-year-old in
Mission 1-1 is the senior gatekeeper in 10-1 and the soon-to-be CEO in
10-2. Every scenario after Level 5 assumes the protagonist now sees the
patterns the earlier levels taught.

### The five-act shape

| Act | Levels | What the protagonist learns |
|---|---|---|
| **I. See the game** | L1–L2 | Pattern recognition. Information discipline. The realisation that small inconsistencies are data, not noise. |
| **II. Hold ground** | L3–L4 | Boundary warfare. Refusing the stage. DARVO recognition. Saying no without a justification. |
| **III. Become gravity** | L5 | Mastery. The shift from defence to presence. Lennox enters as a rival you'll later recruit. |
| **IV. Apply** | L6–L8 | Career (Marcus, Kaya), dating (Elias, Nova), and family (the hardest — The Mother). |
| **V. Endgame** | L9–L10 | The long game (Aria's six-month covert campaign), then the gatekeeper turn, then the inheritance. |

### Voice rules for the inner-voice line

- Always tactical. Never therapeutic.
- One sentence preferred. Two when the second is the punchline.
- Never apologise. Never soften.
- Specific over abstract — *"Both of those are today's data"* over *"That's a red flag."*
- Prefer present tense, declarative.

## Female-track character roster

### MARIS — *the master antagonist*
The dark psychology archetype. Wealthy, charming, terrifying. Recurs
three times across the track; each return is a different vector.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **1-2** | Gala morning-after. First contact. The compliment that's an extraction. | Reading flattery as reconnaissance. |
| **3-2** *(The Guilt Loop)* | 10:32 PM DM, six weeks of silence broken with manufactured vulnerability. | Refusing rescue work for someone performing fragility. The "11pm vulnerability that can wait until 9am wasn't real" rule. |
| **5-1** *(Mastery)* | Returns as an industry contemporary. You're no longer her target — you're her equal, then her counterweight. | The shift from being-played to being-gravity. |

**Maris arc summary:** predator → guilt-trip return → neutralised
through your own ascent. She doesn't lose; she becomes irrelevant.
The lesson is that mastery is the only true defence — you can't
out-argue someone like Maris, you can only out-grow her.

**Unwritten beats / opportunities:**
- A late-track Maris cameo where she tries to recruit *you* as her ally.
- A Maris-mentions-you scene where someone reports back what she's
  said. You see her playbook deployed against you in absentia.

---

### KAYA — *the senior mentor → retiring sovereign*
The protagonist's mirror across the time-skip. Where Maris is the
threat that grows, Kaya is the example that ages. Appears in four
scenarios spanning seven in-fiction years.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **6-1** *(Credit Thief)* | Sets you up at the boardroom — asks the question she knows you can answer. | Senior witnesses as setup artists. |
| **9-1** | Works behind the scenes in Aria's covert campaign. Tells you what's circulating. | Intel before response. Allies as scouts. |
| **9-2** *(Counter-Campaign)* | Coffee-break check-in after the panel attack. *"You passed the first test. What's next?"* | Discipline of not-engaging. The decider matters more than the audience. |
| **10-2** *(The Inheritance)* | Dinner. Asks who you'd nominate for her role. | The eclipse calculation. Naming a successor who'll surpass you. |

**Kaya arc summary:** gentle hand → witness → confidante → retiring
sovereign passing the seat. She models the protagonist's own future.
The dinner in 10-2 is the structural mirror of the gala in 1-1: same
stakes (entry to a tier), opposite end of the table.

**Voice notes:** Kaya talks in finished sentences, never repeats
herself, ends conversations a quarter-second before they need to end.
Her *"Good answer"* in 10-2 is a two-word sentence that changes a
career. Write her sparingly.

### LENNOX — *rival → ally → successor*
The most ambitious arc in the catalogue. Three appearances, each a
different relationship to the protagonist.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **5-2** | Tries the Maris playbook on you. You see it instantly because you've now lived it. | Mastery test. Recognition under pressure. |
| **(L7-9 cameos, opportunity)** | Currently absent from these levels — could appear as a peer at industry events. |
| **10-2** *(The Inheritance)* | Named as the optimal pick for Kaya's role. Will outperform you within five years. | The eclipse calculation. |

**Lennox arc summary:** enemy → recruit → potential successor. The
moment in 5-2 where you choose to recruit her instead of break her is
the seed for the 10-2 nomination. Worth seeding her into 7-1 or 9-1
as a peer-equivalent so her 10-2 appearance carries seven years of
in-fiction history rather than two appearances.

**Unwritten beats / opportunities:**
- L7 cameo: you see her at a panel, both nodding politely at each other.
- L9 cameo: she sends you intel about Aria, unsolicited. The recruitment
  paid forward.

### PRIYA — *the loyal ally*
The trustworthy friend. Appears in twelve scenarios — the most of
anyone. Her consistency is the point.

| Scenario | Beat |
|---|---|
| **1-1** | Coffee meet, the first real ally moment. |
| **2-1, 2-2** | Group chat texture. Won't betray. |
| **3-1, 3-2** | Sounding board for boundary work. |
| **4-1, 4-2** *(DARVO)* | Physical intervention — the shoulder-to-shoulder moment. *"Avery. Not here."* |
| **5-1, 5-2** | Watches you become gravity. |
| **7-1, 7-2** | Dating-strategy peer. |
| **9-1** | Confirms what's in the air about Aria. |
| **10-1** | Fades out as you ascend. (Could appear as the friend you call after Devon leaves.) |

**Priya arc summary:** the only character whose role doesn't change.
She's the constant. Her consistency is the contrast that makes
everyone else's manipulation visible.

**Voice notes:** Priya speaks in short clean sentences, never asks
performative questions, never gives advice unless asked. *"Avery. Not
here."* is the tonal centre — two words and a name, said the way
someone tired of a recurring nuisance would say it.

### ALEX — *the info-fisher friend*
The well-meaning friend who can't keep secrets. Not malicious — undisciplined.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **1-1** *(The Morning After)* | Saturday morning ambush about the gala. Wants details. | Information discipline. Downplay over exaggerate. |
| **2-1** | Group chat where Alex gossips even after being asked not to. | The gap between intent and behaviour with leaky people. |

**Alex arc summary:** static. He's the test, repeating. The protagonist's
relationship to him changes — early scenes she trusts him, later she
deflects automatically.

---

### AVERY — *the DARVO antagonist*
Public-facing manipulator. Builds a persona of injured party.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **4-1** | Smear setup. Subtle accusations in mutual circles. | Recognising the smear before it crystallises. |
| **4-2** *(DARVO)* | Crowded event ambush. Full Deny-Attack-Reverse sequence. | Refusing the stage. The landmine line. |

**Avery arc summary:** opens with a smear, escalates to public attack,
disappears after she fails. Could return in L8/L9 as a colleague who
won't go away — but the cleaner story is to let her exit and have
*Aria* be the new manipulator with different tactics.

### MARCUS — *the credit thief director*
Workplace nemesis. Director-level, controls your reviews.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **6-1** *(The Credit Thief)* | Boardroom theft. Says "the team" through the slides and "I" in the Q&A. | Early attribution. Technical ambush. Documentation posture. |
| **6-2** | Continues — pattern compounds. |

**Marcus arc summary:** the live-action Maris of the workplace. Same
extraction logic, different venue. The 6-1 / 6-2 pair is the workplace
equivalent of the 3-2 / 5-1 pair in the personal arc.

---

### THE_MOTHER + GOLDEN_SIBLING — *the family arc*
The hardest opponents. You can't quit family the way you can quit
Marcus. Two scenarios, no clean wins.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **8-1** | The 29-year-old script. Established roles you didn't choose. | Recognising scripts that long-predate your awareness of them. |
| **8-2** | The weaponised letter. Written communication that becomes evidence. | Documentation cuts both ways. Letters are receipts. |

**Family arc summary:** no good endings, only better ones. The
emotional cost is higher than any other level. Should remain the
darkest scenarios in tone — the only level where the protagonist
might genuinely cry on the page.

---

### ARIA — *the long-game opponent*
Covert for six months, then public for three weeks. The endgame
antagonist before you take Kaya's seat.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **9-1** | The whispers. Six months of plausibly-deniable doubt-planting. | Recognising covert campaigns. Counter-intel. |
| **9-2** *(Counter-Campaign)* | Public stage attack. Three weeks before the role decision. | Refusing the stage. Quiet meeting beats public rebuttal. Work as the final argument. |

**Aria arc summary:** the most strategic antagonist. Where Avery
performs hostility, Aria performs neutrality. The discipline of not
engaging her is the entire L9 lesson.

---

### DEVON — *the protégé*
Appears late, but consequentially. The next generation.

| Scenario | Beat | Tactic taught |
|---|---|---|
| **10-1** *(The Gatekeeper)* | The intro request. You decide whether to be the mentor you wanted or the no you got. | Conditional access. Specific feedback as true generosity. |
| **10-2** *(The Inheritance)* | Considered as Kaya's-role nominee. Loyalty-pick that fails the eclipse test. | Distinguishing loyalty from judgment. |

**Devon arc summary:** the inverse of the protagonist. Where the
protagonist fought to be let through doors, Devon is who you become
the door for. The 10-1 → 10-2 pair is the "you became Kaya" arc made
literal.

## Book tactics — explicit references

The simulator teaches the same tactics as *The Sociopathic Dating
Bible*. When writing new scenes, reach for the book's vocabulary
deliberately. Common tactic taxonomy:

| Tactic | Where it lives in the simulator |
|---|---|
| **Information discipline** | 1-1 (Alex), 6-1 (Marcus reframe) |
| **Pattern recognition** | 1-2 (Maris compliment), 9-1 (Aria whispers) |
| **The Doctrine of Cold** | 3-2 (Maris guilt-loop), 4-2 (DARVO refuse) |
| **Frame control** | 4-2 (refuse the stage), 6-1 (correct attribution before frame sets) |
| **Implied receipts** | 4-2 (the email-thread landmine), 6-1 (documentation posture) |
| **Exit at peak** | 4-2 (audience-pivoted), 9-2 (walk-out timing) |
| **Allies as windows** | 4-2 (Priya intervenes), 6-1 (Kaya's setup question) |
| **Quiet documentation > public correction** | 6-1 (after-the-meeting), 9-2 (hiring-meeting beat Aria's 6 months of whispers) |
| **Work as the final argument** | 9-2 (ignore-and-ship → ending-work-closed) |
| **The eclipse calculation** | 10-2 (Lennox nomination) |
| **The voice you needed** | 10-1 (Devon mentor path) |
| **Mastery as gravity** | 5-1 (you stop being a target), 10-2 (the room re-orients around you) |

When introducing a new scenario, pick *one* primary tactic and let
the secondary choices teach the failure modes of that tactic.
Every scene should have an inner-voice line that names the
tactic being deployed (against you OR by you).

## Voice consistency checklist

Before shipping a scenario, scan it for:

- [ ] Every scene with choices has at least one inner-voice line
      framing the choice tactically.
- [ ] No character apologises in their own dialogue. Manipulators
      don't apologise; allies don't need to.
- [ ] At least one scene includes a small visual detail (the way
      someone holds a folder, a quarter-degree head-tilt, the wine
      glass being lifted three inches off the tablecloth).
- [ ] The optimal choice's tactic line names the principle, not
      the consequence.
- [ ] Endings have a one-line inner-voice closer that lands the
      lesson without lecturing.

## Continuity bibles

The following are canon and should never be contradicted in new
content:

- **The gala** is the prologue of the female track. Anyone the
  protagonist met there (Maris, Dana, Priya, the unknown number)
  is a thread the player remembers from 1-1.
- **Maris's playbook** is consistent across her three appearances:
  open with flattery → manufacture intimacy → extract → ghost or
  guilt-trip when the extraction stalls.
- **Kaya's age vs the protagonist's**: Kaya is ~12 years older.
  She's retiring at the end of L10, so she enters L6 in her early
  fifties.
- **Lennox's recruitment in 5-2** is the seed for 10-2. Don't
  contradict the lunch they had together.
- **The senior role** in L9-L10 is the same role. Aria attacks for
  it in 9-2; the protagonist gets it; Kaya's role-above sits one
  tier higher, and that's what 10-2's nomination is for.
