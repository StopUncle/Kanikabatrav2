## Logline

You've been dating Reese for four months. Tonight, you finally meet "the group" - five friends who've known each other since college. By midnight, you'll know if you passed their tests... and whether you even want to.

---

## Core Theme

**The social machinery of manipulation.** This scenario exposes how friend groups can become:
- Extensions of a manipulator's control
- Information networks (reporting your behavior)
- Loyalty testing grounds
- Vehicles for triangulation
- Social proof of "you're the problem"

The hardest part of leaving some relationships isn't the partner - it's the entire social world built around them.

---

## Psychology Being Taught

### Flying Monkey Dynamics
Friends who serve the manipulator's agenda, consciously or not:
- **The Reporter**: Tells Reese everything you say/do
- **The Enforcer**: Punishes deviation from group norms
- **The Triangulator**: Creates jealousy and competition
- **The Validator**: Constantly confirms Reese's narrative
- **The Recruiter**: Love-bombs you into the fold

### Group Manipulation Tactics

1. **The Loyalty Test**: Situations designed to see whose "side" you're on
2. **The Inside Joke Freeze-Out**: Using shared history to exclude
3. **The Information Extraction**: Casual questions that get reported back
4. **The Comparison Game**: Subtle mentions of exes or "better" behavior
5. **The Group Opinion**: "We all think..." as pressure
6. **The Conditional Inclusion**: Warmth when compliant, ice when not
7. **The Triangulation Setup**: Creating jealousy through "harmless" flirting
8. **The Exit Block**: "Don't break up the group" pressure

### Key Lesson
Healthy friend groups welcome new people. Toxic ones test them. The difference is whether you feel like you're auditioning... or arriving.

## Structure Overview

### Part 1: The Arrival (Scenes 1-12)
First meeting with the full group. The tests begin immediately.

**Key Moments**:
- Initial warmth that feels like an interview
- First loyalty test (Reese leaves you alone with them)
- Information extraction attempts
- Ellis triangulation setup
- Finding Avery as potential ally

### Part 2: The Deep End (Scenes 13-24)
You're "in" now - but the tests intensify.

**Key Moments**:
- Group trip or activity where you can't escape
- Kai reports something you said; Reese reacts
- Morgan delivers explicit warning
- Group peer pressure on a decision
- Avery confides (or doesn't)

### Part 3: The Reckoning (Scenes 25-36)
Choice point: Assimilate, resist, or leave.

**Key Moments**:
- Major loyalty test (Reese vs. your outside life)
- Confrontation with group or Reese
- Exit attempt (and how group responds)
- Resolution

## Voice Guide Summary

### Reese
- Charming in public, cooler in private about group issues
- Uses "we" and "the group" as authority
- Gets cold when friends report problems
- Frames everything as "you fitting in" or not

### Morgan
- Direct, minimal warmth until you're proven
- Uses silence as power
- Threatens through "warnings"
- Respects you if you push back correctly (rare)

### Kai
- Never stops being friendly
- Questions feel innocent but aren't
- Reports everything immediately
- "Just curious!" is the tell

### Ellis
- Flirty is the weapon
- History with Reese is always mentioned
- Tests your jealousy threshold
- Cold when you don't play the game

### Avery
- Quiet observations
- Speaks carefully (group is always listening)
- Only opens up in private
- The one who might understand

### Inner Voice
- Notices when warmth feels like evaluation
- Questions the group dynamics
- Gets quieter in bad endings
- More direct in good endings
