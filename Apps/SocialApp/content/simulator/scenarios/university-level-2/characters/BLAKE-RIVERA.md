## Clinical Profile

### Attachment Style (Secure with Caveats)
- Comfortable with intimacy and independence
- Generally trusting, able to depend on others
- Consistent, reliable presence
- Handles conflict constructively
- BUT: Subtly competitive with player
- Hidden insecurity about being "the sidekick"

### Core Tension
**Genuine loyalty** vs **subtle competition**
Blake genuinely wants player to succeed... as long as player doesn't succeed MORE than him.

## Dialogue Analysis

### Voice Patterns

#### Supportive Mode (Default)
```
"I got your back."
"What do you need?"
"That was rough. You okay?"
"We'll figure this out together."
```
- Reliable, grounding
- Action-oriented support
- Checks in on player
- "We" language

#### Competitive Undertone Mode (Subtle)
```
"Nice move. I was thinking the same thing."
"Good job! I mean, I would've done it differently, but..."
"Lucky break, huh?"
"You're really getting the hang of this. Eventually."
```
- Backhanded compliments
- "I would have" comparisons
- Attributes success to luck
- Subtle minimization

#### Out of Depth Mode (Level 3)
```
"These people are... a lot."
"Did I say something wrong?"
"I don't know if I belong here."
"Just tell me what to do."
```
- Overwhelmed by elite world
- Defers to player
- Anxiety shows through
- May overcompensate

#### Crisis Mode (Level 4)
```
"I don't know what to do."
"They're asking me to... I can't."
"What would you do?"
"I'm scared."
```
- Vulnerability emerges
- Needs support (role reversal)
- Loyalty at breaking point
- Real stakes

## The Competition Dynamic

### How It Manifests
- Takes credit for shared ideas
- "Lucky" framing for player's wins
- Subtle one-upmanship
- Slightly late with congratulations
- Works harder when player succeeds

### Teaching Function
Even good friends can have competitive edges. Recognizing this doesn't mean ending friendship - it means managing it.

### Red Flags to Watch
1. Backhanded compliments
2. "I was going to suggest that" after your idea works
3. More enthusiastic about your failures than successes
4. Compares himself favorably in subtle ways
