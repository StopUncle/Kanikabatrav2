- Comfortable with intimacy and independence

## Dialogue Analysis

### Voice Patterns

#### Warm Mode (Default)
```
"You must be exhausted. These events are draining."
"I'm Millicent. Maris's sister. The other one."
"Can I get you anything? Water? A moment of sanity?"
```
- Genuine warmth
- Self-deprecating humor
- Offers real help
- No agenda (rare in this world)

#### Concerned Mode
```
"I've seen how she looks at you. Be careful."
"Maris... she's complicated. That's the family word for it."
"I worry about the people she brings into her orbit."
```
- Protective instinct
- Knows Maris's nature
- Tries to warn without explicit betrayal
- Conflicted loyalty

#### Revealing Mode (Rare)
```
"We're twins. Same face. Same DNA. But..."
"There was a moment. When we were young. Something broke in her."
"I keep hoping I'll see it come back. Whatever she lost."
```
- Deep family insight
- Mourns the sister she doesn't have
- Genuine grief
- Still loves Maris (despite everything)

#### Direct Mode
```
"She will use you. That's what she does."
"I'm not her. Please remember that."
"If you need an ally who doesn't want anything... I'm here."
```
- Clarity about Maris's nature
- Distinguishes herself
- Offers genuine help
- No strings attached

## Function in Story

### Teaching Role
Shows that:
1. Not everyone in this world is manipulative
2. Genuine people exist (and struggle)
3. Family dynamics can split two identical people
4. Warning signs can come from unexpected allies

### Contrast Function
- Warmth vs Maris's calculated charm
- Genuine concern vs strategic vulnerability
- Real help vs transactional favor
- Grief for loss vs exploitation of weakness

## Red Flags Taught (Inverse)

Millicent teaches by CONTRAST - what genuine looks like:

1. **Warmth that persists** = not performance
2. **Help without conditions** = rare but real
3. **Same genetics, different outcome** = nature AND nurture
4. **Warnings without agenda** = genuine concern
5. **Grief for family** = love despite knowing
